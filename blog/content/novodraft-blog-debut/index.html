More Human Than Human

Posted on: Mon Apr 15 2024

We have a (very) unofficial slogan here: more human than human.

OK, sure, it's a line from the movie Blade Runner... a movie where, admittedly, things didn't end well in an AI-enhanced future. We found the film fascinating when we first saw it back in the 80s.

If nothing else, the film and Thomas Pynchon's story "Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep" (on which the film was based) have kept ruminations on the potential – and risks – of advanced technology on our minds.

Yep, we've been thinking about this stuff since it was solely the provenance of sci-fi flicks. And that is quite a while.

But we digresss. To us, the slogan means providing users with litigation-ready documents that could be drafted by practitioners at the profession's highest levels. (This is the “human” at the end of the motto.) But also, providing them with a speed and ease that far exceeds the capabilities of a human legal writer. (That's the “more human than” part.)

With this post, we explicate with greater particularity what that means.

We recently spotted an article on The Register, an enterprise technology newsletter, that focused on a growing problem with AI content. Specifically, the problem of vacuous, low value “AI spam” and its negative effect on the Internet as a whole.

In the article, Jon Gillham, founder and CEO of AI content detection platform Originality.ai, told The Register that “Google is losing its war on spammy, scammy content in its search results.”

Granted, as the founder of an "AI Gotcha" platform, Gillham's got skin in the game, but that doesn't make him wrong.

In fact, his thesis has been echoed in other respected publications.

"Not all AI content is spam, but I think right now all spam is AI content." Gillham told The Register.

That brings to mind a very old saying (the first recorded use dates back to 1957.) amongst software engineers: garbage in, garbage out. It means that, in computing, poor quality input will always produce faulty output. That is what we are beginning to see, and its what we believe will determine the winners and losers debuting this first wave of AI applications.

At Novodraft, we believe that AI generative content is only as good as the human prompt (AI) engineers and gatekeepers that guide its creation and deployment. AI is not a “set it and forget it” proposition, one that can be "switched on”, enabling its owners to retire to a beach in the Caribbean and collect checks.

Rather, the process that yields AI generative content depends on active management and intelligent, informed curation.

The most integral part of Novodraft are the human attorneys and engineers behind it. Attorneys with a track record of reliability, trial wins, and scholarly publication... who also happen to have software/systems engineering backgrounds. Their efforts are the input.

It is what sets Novodraft apart.

Demonstrating this belief is one of the purposes of this blog. We challenge you to spot a post that is AI-generated, versus one written by our staff. In the future, we'll offer incentives for doing so successfully.

In the meantime, we challenge you to spot an "all AI" post. If you think you have one, let us know. If you're right, we will tell you so, point-blank, without hesitation. And you'll know whether you can rely on Novodraft to be more human than human.
